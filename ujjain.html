About Ujjain : Spiritual Capital of Madhya Pradesh

Ujjain, a historic city in Madhya Pradesh, is renowned for its rich cultural heritage and ancient temples. As one of the oldest cities in India, Ujjain holds immense significance as a sacred pilgrimage site and a center of astronomy, spirituality, and religious traditions.

Ujjain has been known by several names throughout history, including Avantika, Ujjayini, and Kundanpur. Its origin can be traced back to ancient times, with references found in Hindu scriptures, Buddhist texts, and Jain literature.

The city is considered one of the seven holiest places in Hinduism, known as Sapta Puri. It is revered as the location where Lord Shiva defeated the demon Tripurasura, earning the city its name "Ujjayini," meaning "victorious."

Ujjain is also famous for hosting the Simhastha Kumbh Mela, a massive Hindu pilgrimage that occurs every 12 years. Millions of devotees gather here to participate in the holy bathing rituals at the sacred Shipra River.

Visitors can explore iconic landmarks like the Mahakaleshwar Jyotirlinga, one of the twelve Jyotirlingas in India, and the Kal Bhairav Temple, where devotees offer liquor to the deity. Additionally, Ujjain is home to the historic Jantar Mantar observatory, showcasing ancient astronomical instruments. With its blend of spirituality, history, and architectural marvels, Ujjain offers a unique and enriching travel experience.
